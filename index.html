This course begins with matrix arithmetic. Understanding matrices as linear transformations of Euclidean spaces, we then naturally transition into the study of linear spaces and linear mappings. Finally, we introduce the concept of the determinant of a matrix.

Advanced Linear Algebra II (H) (MA121)

Prof. Ziqing Xiang Personal homepage School's homepage
Spring, 2024

This course is divided into three parts. The first part is about some preliminaries and basic algebraic objects:

  • Definitions and examples of group, ring and field.
  • Definitions and examples of module over a ring, and review of several results in ALAI in the language of module.
  • Equivalent relation and quotient.
  • Polynomials and polynomial ring.
  • Dual and double dual.
  • Tensor, symmetric and exterior power.
The second part is about bilinear form, definiteness and inner product:
  • Bilinear form and bilinear space.
  • Sesquilinear form and quadratic form.
  • Positive definiteness, positive semi-definiteness, negative definiteness and negative semi-definiteness.
  • Inner product and adjoint operator.
  • Diagonalization and SVD.
  • Optimization.
The third part is about Jordan-Chevalley decomposition.
  • A toy model.
  • Generalized eigenspace.
  • Semisimple and nilpotent operator.
  • Jordan-Chevalley decomposition..
  • Jordan normal form and matrix exponential.
  • Modules over PID.

Grade: A+ (97)
Language: English, Chinese(only for talking language)
Textbook: Instructor-prepared Materials
Type: Required
Credit: 4

Mathematical Analysis I, II, III (MA101a, MA102a, MA203a)

Prof. MA Fuming (Retired), Prof. Yunhao Fu School's homepage
Fall, 2023; Spring, 2024; Fall, 2024

Introduction to basic mathematical analysis.

  • Limits and series of sequences and functions.
  • Continuity, differentiability, series of functions.
  • Integration, Lebesgue measure and theorem, applications of integration.
  • Incomplete integral, including integral with parameters。
  • Point set topology of Euclidean space.
  • Muti-variables function and its continuity, differentiability, integration.
  • Integration along curves and surfaces. Guass formula and Stokes formula.
  • Mathematics of field in Physics.
Additional topics on elementary Fourier analysis.

Grade: A-(91), A+(97), A(94)
Language: Chinese
Textbook: 《数学分析教程》（上下册）常庚哲，史济怀 (A Course in Mathematical Analysis I, II by CHANG Gengzhe and SHI Jihuai)
Type: Required
Credit: 5, 5, 5
