Advanced Linear Algebra II (H)
Prof. XIANG Ziqing   Personal Homepage   MathSciNet   Faculty Page
MA121, Spring, 2024
This course is divided into three parts. The first part is preliminaries and basic algebraic objects:
  • Group, ring, and field.
  • Modules over a ring. Review advanced linear algebra I in the module language.
  • Equivalent relation and quotient set.
  • Polynomial ring.
  • Dual and double dual.
  • Tensor algebra and symmetric/exterior power.
The second part is bilinear form, definiteness, and inner product, which is the classical contexts of advanced linear algebra II:
  • Bilinear/Sesquilinear/Quadratic form/space.
  • Positive/Nagative (semi-)definiteness
  • Adjoint operator.
  • Diagonalization and singular value decomposition(SVD).
  • Optimization.
The third part is Jordan-Chevalley decomposition. The instructor provides a toy module as a motivation and a high view point of the decomposition:
  • A toy model.
  • Generalized eigenvalue/eigenvector/eigenspace.
  • Semisimple/Nilpotent operator.
  • Jordan-Chevalley decomposition..
  • Jordan normal form and matrix exponential.
  • Modules over a principal ideal domain(PID).
Grade A+ (97)
Textbook Instructor-prepared Materials
Language English, Chinese
Type Required
Credit 4
